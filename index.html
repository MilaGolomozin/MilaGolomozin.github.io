Drug Crime in San Francisco

Drug-related crime has been a central issue in California politics. But how much of what we see in the data reflects policy — and how much reflects deeper structural change?

1. Changes Over Time

We begin by examining how drug-related crimes have evolved over the past two decades.

There is a sharp drop between 2009–2011, followed by a rise after 2021 and another spike around 2025.

While we cannot claim causation, these shifts align with major policy periods and suggest possible influence.

2. A Shift in Where Crime Happens

Total crime counts alone do not tell the full story. We now turn to geography.

Over time, drug crime becomes increasingly concentrated in Tenderloin, Southern, and Mission.

What was once more distributed across the city is now localized.

3. Comparing with Other Crimes

Is this pattern unique to drug crime, or part of a broader trend?

Other crimes remain relatively stable in their geographic distribution.

This suggests the concentration is specific to drug-related activity.

Conclusion

Drug crime in San Francisco has changed not just in scale, but in structure.

The increasing spatial concentration points to deeper dynamics beyond overall crime levels.
