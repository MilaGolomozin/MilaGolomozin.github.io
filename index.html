Drug Crime in San Francisco

Drug-related crime has been a central issue in California politics. But how much of what we see in the data reflects policy — and how much reflects deeper structural change?

1. Changes Over Time

We begin by looking at how drug-related crimes have changed over the past two decades.

There is a sharp drop between 2009–2011, followed by a rise after 2021 and a spike around 2025.

While we cannot claim causation, the timing suggests that policy may play a role in shaping these trends.

But overall trends don’t tell us where crime happens. To understand that, we need to look at geography.

2. A Shift in Where Crime Happens

The map below shows how the spatial distribution of drug-related crime has changed over time.

Drug crime becomes increasingly concentrated in Tenderloin, Southern, and Mission.

What was once distributed across the city is now localized in key areas.

Is this concentration unique to drug crime, or part of a broader trend?

3. Comparing with Other Crimes

We compare drug crime with other high-frequency crimes such as larceny/theft.

Other crimes remain relatively stable in their geographic distribution over time.

This suggests the concentration we observe is specific to drug-related activity.

Conclusion

Drug crime in San Francisco has changed not just in scale, but in structure.

While trends may align with policy shifts, the growing spatial concentration points to deeper underlying dynamics.
